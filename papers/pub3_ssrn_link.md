**Title:** Adaptive Tokenomics: A Systems Engineering Approach to Programmable Incentive Design

**Author:** Zach Zukowski

## Abstract

This paper presents a systems engineering framework for designing self-regulating token economies in Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks (DePIN). We develop a five-mechanism simulation — productive staking, burn-mint equilibrium, PID-controlled adaptive emission, reputation-weighted governance, and conviction voting — calibrated against empirical data from Helium, Filecoin, Geodnet, and Ethereum validator economics. Across 806 simulation runs spanning four macro scenarios and 30-seed ensembles, PID-controlled emission compresses worst-case node-count variance by 6.25x relative to static emission under competitor shock. The model demonstrates that adaptive emission provides a stabilization channel analogous to automatic fiscal stabilizers in traditional monetary economics. We identify a non-monotonicity in PID integral gain that creates a "Goldilocks zone" for controller tuning and document how conviction voting with appropriate decay parameters resists whale capture up to 40% token concentration.
